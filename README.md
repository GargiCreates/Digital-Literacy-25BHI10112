Digital Literacy 
Project Overview

This project explores the concept of digital literacy and its importance in modern student life. It highlights the gap between simply using digital tools and truly understanding their impact, risks, and ethical responsibilities.

The project focuses on how students interact with digital platforms for learning, communication, and collaboration, while often lacking awareness of digital systems, online behavior, and cyber safety.

 Objectives
Understand the meaning of digital literacy beyond basic skills
Analyze digital identity, ethics, and online behavior
Evaluate collaboration tools and their impact on learning
Identify cyber risks and preventive measures
Improve digital communication and etiquette

 Key Learnings
Digital literacy is not just technical skill, but responsibility and awareness
Online actions create a permanent digital footprint
Collaboration tools improve learning but require ethical usage
Cyber safety depends on both user behavior and system design
Professional communication impacts opportunities and perception
 Cyber Safety Awareness
Avoid phishing links and suspicious messages
Use strong passwords and enable 2FA
Never share OTPs or UPI PIN
Report cyber crimes at: https://cybercrime.gov.in
Helpline: 1930
 Conclusion

This project helped shift the perspective from simply using digital tools to using them responsibly and thoughtfully. It emphasizes ethical behavior, safe practices, and professional communication in digital environments.
